Nutritional Framework

The Raw
Primal Diet

Not a trend. Not an ideology. A return to the nutritional density that built the human brain — animal-sourced, unprocessed, bioavailable at a level that plant-based or cooked food cannot approximate. The raw primal framework is the foundation upon which all other Regenesis protocols operate.

01

Bioavailability First

Raw animal foods contain nutrients in their most bioavailable form — enzymes intact, cofactors preserved, protein structures undenatured. Cooking destroys what the body needs most.

02

Organ Meat Primacy

Muscle meat is peripheral tissue. The organs — liver, heart, brain, kidney, adrenal — contain the highest concentrations of vitamins, minerals, and metabolic cofactors in nature.

03

Fat as Primary Fuel

The brain is 60% fat. Ancestral humans ran predominantly on fat metabolism. Raw saturated fat from ruminant animals is not a risk — it is the substrate of cognitive coherence.

04

Like Feeds Like

The principle of organotherapy: consuming the tissue of an organ provides the concentrated raw material that organ requires to rebuild and function. Brain for brain. Heart for heart.

The Science

Grounded
in mechanism.

Every protocol in Regenesis is built from biological first principles — not from observational trends or commercial interest. The mechanisms are understood. The evidence is in your physiology.

Bioavailability of Raw Animal Foods

The enzymatic content of raw meat and organs significantly enhances protein digestion and nutrient absorption compared to cooked equivalents. Heat denaturing eliminates heat-labile cofactors including B12 binding proteins, certain enzyme complexes, and growth factors present in raw tissue.

Organ Meats as Nutritional Apex

Gram for gram, liver contains more bioavailable nutrients than any other food — including retinol (vitamin A), B12, folate, copper, zinc, CoQ10, and carnitine. These are not supplements; they are the original cellular fuel in their most complete form.

Neuroplasticity & Nutritional Substrates

BDNF (Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor) synthesis requires adequate DHA, cholesterol, and B-vitamin cofactors — all found in concentrated form in raw animal foods. The correlation between ancestral fat-rich diets and cognitive architecture expansion is not coincidental.

Autophagy & Cellular Senescence

Strategic fasting periods trigger autophagy — the cellular self-cleaning process that clears damaged proteins and dysfunctional organelles. This mechanism is fundamental to longevity and is enhanced by a fat-adapted metabolic state.
