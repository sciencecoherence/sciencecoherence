Agmatine Sulfate

I. The Pharmacological Identity

Agmatine Sulfate is a metabolite of L-Arginine, but calling it "just another Arginine" is a massive understatement. While Arginine is a precursor, Agmatine is the signaling molecule itself. It is stored in neurons and released during activation, functioning as a true neurotransmitter.

NOS Modulation

Agmatine is unique: it inhibits iNOS and nNOS (associated with inflammation and neurotoxicity) while stimulating eNOS (endothelial), which is responsible for vasodilation and the famous "muscle pump".

NMDA Antagonism

It acts as a mild NMDA receptor antagonist. This prevents "glutamate storms" and helps maintain the brain's internal balance against over-excitation.

Imidazoline Agonism

By hitting Imidazoline receptors, it may support insulin sensitivity and blood pressure regulation, acting as a metabolic "shunter" for nutrients.
