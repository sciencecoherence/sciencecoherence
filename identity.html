05
Motion & Texture
The site breathes slowly. Motion is deliberate — emergence, not performance.

Grain Overlay

The skin of the site

A subtle noise texture at 2–4% opacity sits above everything. It prevents the dark backgrounds from feeling digital or synthetic — the grain gives the void organic depth, like parchment or a dark mineral stone.

Ambient Glow

Warmth emerging from dark

Large, diffuse radial gradients in amber and moss at 5–8% opacity create the sensation of warmth radiating from within the page. Never applied as surface decoration — always as atmospheric depth.

Entry Animations

Slow emergence

Elements enter with a slow fade-up on a cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1) curve — quick acceleration, long deceleration. Staggered delays of 150–200ms between elements. The sensation is of something becoming visible, not something moving.

Hover States

Alive, not reactive

Hover interactions use long transition durations (400–600ms) and avoid abrupt state changes. Branch tiles glow softly inward when hovered. Navigation links shift color temperature rather than weight. Transitions feel like breath, not clicks.

07
Design Principles
The rules that govern every visual decision made within this system.

Awe Through Restraint

The feeling of encountering something larger than oneself does not come from visual complexity — it comes from scale, silence, and negative space deployed with absolute intention. The void is not empty; it is full of possibility.

Warmth Emerges, Never Decorates

Amber, parchment, and bone never sit on top of the dark. They appear to radiate from within it — as if the content itself is bioluminescent. Applied as gradients, glows, and text; never as fills or blocks.

Typography as Architecture

Playfair Display in italic carries the intellectual weight of a printed manuscript. Cinzel brings the permanence of stone inscription. Every typographic choice reinforces that this work has been built to last.

Three Colors, One System

The branch colors — green, cerulean, gold — never compete. They signal distinct territories within the same unified field. No branch dominates the others visually; the hierarchy is structural, not chromatic.

The Orbital as Recurring Motif

The logo mark — cell or atom or solar system — appears throughout the site in varying scales and opacities. It is the visual metaphor for coherence: discrete elements in organized, living relationship.

Grain Grounds Everything

The noise texture layer is non-negotiable. It prevents the interface from feeling clinical or digital. The grain is the site's skin — organic, tactile, present. Without it, the warmth becomes decorative rather than felt.
