NEURO-DYNAMICS

THE NERVOUS SYSTEM AS A LAYERED PROTOCOL

This page is a map of how the full Regenesis stack interacts with the brain and behavior over time. Micro-dosing is only one lane. The structure is layered: daily foundations, weekly modulation, and cyclic deep interventions. The goal is simple: stable baseline, clean signal, durable change.

01. THE STRUCTURE

Three layers, one nervous system

Think in layers, not “magic bullets”. Daily behaviors set the baseline. Weekly sessions tune perception and emotional processing. Cyclic deep interventions can reset patterns, but only if the integration layer is protected.

OUTPUT: stability OUTPUT: clarity OUTPUT: consolidation
Integration rule
The stronger the intervention, the more important the “quiet days” around it. Your protocol works when the nervous system gets time to save changes: sleep, nutrition, low chaos, repetition.

04. CADENCE MAP

Daily foundations
Baseline layer
  • Sleep consistency and evening downshift
  • Nutrition and gut stability as “neural power supply”
  • Low-chaos routines that reduce interpretation volatility
Cyclic iboga flood intervention
Deep reset layer
  • High-impact lane that can reorganize behavior and emotional architecture
  • Integration window is not optional: rest, stable food, low stimulus, support
  • Medical screening matters (cardiac risk is not theoretical)
Red flags to respect
This lane should be treated as high-risk and supervised. Cardiac screening and electrolyte status are common safety priorities. If your body says “not now”, the protocol listens.
Weekly psilocybin session
Modulation layer
  • Used as a structured “recalibration” lane, not entertainment
  • Works best when paired with integration: journaling, nature, gentle cognition
  • Protect sleep and avoid stacking with stimulants on the same day
Safety notes
If there is a history of psychosis, bipolar mania, or severe dissociation, this lane can be risky. Medication interactions are a real thing. If in doubt, treat “supervised and screened” as the default.
Iboga microdosing
Between floods
  • Used as a low-intensity integration support between flood cycles: steadier mood, cleaner drive, less compulsive drift.
  • Keep it bounded and cycled (avoid turning it into a daily dependency pattern).
  • Sleep is the governor: if it increases insomnia, agitation, irritability, or compulsive use, pause and return to stabilization.
Safety notes
Iboga can carry cardiac risk and can interact with medications. Treat screening and supervision as the default. Also avoid stacking it with stimulants, especially near evening.

06. NICOTINIC AS A NEURAL TOOL

TARGET: nAChR / ATTENTIONAL GATING

THE ATTENTION LENS

Nicotine is treated here as an acute attentional gate that can temporarily sharpen signal selection, increase task-lock, and raise cognitive “edge” during output windows. Because it can train dependence, it belongs to tight constraints (short windows, deliberate use, no drift).

Power tool, not lifestyle: it’s most valuable when it stays rare, intentional, and bounded.
